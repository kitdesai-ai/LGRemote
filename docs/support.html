LG webOS Remote

Need help? Most issues come down to the iPhone and TV not being on the same network, or pairing not being accepted on the TV. The answers below cover the common cases. For anything else, email ankitdesai88@gmail.com.

Getting started

  1. Make sure your iPhone and LG TV are on the same Wi-Fi network.
  2. Open the app — it scans your network and lists the LG TVs it finds.
  3. Tap your TV, then accept the pairing prompt on the TV screen.
  4. The pairing key is saved, so future connections are instant.

Frequently asked questions

The app can't find my TV.

Confirm the TV and iPhone are on the same network (not a guest network or a separate 2.4/5 GHz SSID that's isolated). On the TV, enable Settings → Network → LG Connect Apps (sometimes called "Mobile TV On"). Then reopen the app or pull to rescan in Settings. You can also add the TV's IP address manually in Settings.

It found my TV but won't connect / pairing fails.

When you tap the TV, a prompt appears on the TV asking to allow the connection — accept it with your TV remote. If you missed it, tap Reconnect in the app to trigger it again.

Wake-on-LAN doesn't turn my TV on.

Enter your TV's MAC address in Settings (find it under the TV's network info). Wake-on-LAN also requires the TV setting that keeps networking alive in standby — on most LG models this is Settings → General → Mobile TV On (or "Turn on via Wi-Fi" / "Turn on via mobile"). The iPhone must be on the same network for the magic packet to reach the TV.

Which TVs are supported?

LG TVs running webOS, 2014 models and newer.

Does the app collect my data?

No. It talks only to your TV on your local network. See the Privacy Policy.
